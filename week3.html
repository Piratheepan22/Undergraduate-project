Detailed Update

This week, I focused on understanding the Sentinel-2 satellite system, which is part of the Copernicus Programme led by the European Space Agency (ESA). Sentinel-2 consists of a pair of satellites (Sentinel-2A and Sentinel-2B) that provide high-resolution optical imagery for land monitoring. I learned that the satellite captures data in 13 spectral bands, ranging from visible and near-infrared to shortwave infrared, which is highly useful for vegetation analysis, land use mapping, and water quality monitoring. The spatial resolution varies from 10m to 60m depending on the band, and the revisit time is about 5 days, allowing for frequent observation of changes on Earthâ€™s surface. These features make Sentinel-2 an essential tool for environmental monitoring and applications like tracking harmful algal blooms.
