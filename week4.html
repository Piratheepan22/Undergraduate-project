Week 4

Overview

This week, I studied the concept of Linear Unmixing in Hyperspectral Imaging (HSI), which is crucial for analyzing mixed pixels in remote sensing data. This method enables estimation of the proportion of different materials (endmembers) within a single pixel, improving the precision of land and water analysis, especially in detecting harmful algal blooms (HABs).

Detailed Update

I focused on understanding how linear spectral unmixing works and how it can be used to analyze hyperspectral data. This method assumes that the reflectance measured at a pixel is a linear combination of the reflectance of pure substances (endmembers) present in that pixel. The key learning areas included:

  • Understanding the linear mixing model: R=Mâ‹…A+e
  • Where:
    • R = observed reflectance vector
    • M = matrix of endmember spectra
    • A = abundance fractions
    • e = error/residual
  • Learning methods for endmember extraction (e.g., VCA, N-FINDR)
  • Applications of unmixing in environmental monitoring and coastal water analysis

These techniques are highly relevant for breaking down pixel-level information in hyperspectral data to understand what materials are present and in what quantities, especially for HAB monitoring.
