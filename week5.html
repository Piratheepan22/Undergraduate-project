Detailed Update

This week, I studied how Hyperspectral Imaging (HSI) can be applied to detect and analyze slum areas in urban environments, specifically within Sri Lanka. HSI provides high spectral resolution, allowing for the identification of materials and structures based on their unique spectral signaturesâ€”an advantage over traditional multispectral imaging.

In the context of slum detection, HSI helps distinguish informal settlements from planned urban areas based on features such as:

  • Roofing materials (e.g., metal sheets, tarps)
  • Soil conditions
  • Sparse or patchy vegetation
  • Mixed construction patterns
This method is especially useful in rapidly urbanizing regions where informal settlements are hard to detect using standard satellite images.
